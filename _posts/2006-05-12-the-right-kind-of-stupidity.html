This view is reinforced later by an anthropologist who intially characterizes the site’s users as voyeurs and exhibitionists, and indeed that is the conventional view taken of such sites as MySpace and Facebook and 43Things by the media and large parts of the public – the entire Internet is nothing more than a unregulated and pointless updating of We Live in Public. And it’s a fear that even the most confirmed Web2.0 heads wonder if all this filling out data everywhere is really worth it.

But after thinking it over, the anthropologist reconsiders and decides posting content on Facebook is a lot like hanging out in malls. It is not so much an exercise in undirected exhibitionism but an active effort at social connection – albeit without the same direct purpose as dating or professional networking.
